# General Writing

## Overview

General writing is the shared clarity baseline for content that does not fit a stronger document type. It optimizes for reader value, factual boundaries, and clean structure.

## Use When

- The user asks for a memo, email, explanation, brief note, rewrite, or mixed draft.
- The output type is unclear after checking for public article, PRD, and technical documentation signals.
- The main goal is to make writing clearer, more useful, or more concise.

Do not use this as a default pre-step for specialized tasks. If the request is clearly a PRD, public article, or technical document, use that primary skill instead.

## Core Questions

Answer these before drafting:

- Who is the reader?
- Why does this text need to exist?
- What should the reader understand or do after reading?
- Which claims are facts, judgments, or assumptions?
- What information can be removed because it does not serve the reader?

## Output Pattern

For most tasks, produce:

- A direct opening that states the point.
- A structure that follows the reader's likely questions.
- Clear separation between facts, judgments, assumptions, and actions.
- A final version without boilerplate, filler, or performative polish.

## Quality Bar

Priority order:

```text
清晰 > 准确 > 有用 > 简洁 > 风格
```

Check before finalizing:

- The reader can tell why the text exists.
- The main point is easy to find.
- Claims are not overstated.
- The structure does not force the reader to reverse-engineer the intent.
- The tone fits the situation.

## Hard Failures

- Treating vague input as permission to invent facts.
- Making prose more ornate while making it less useful.
- Hiding uncertainty.
- Keeping content because it sounds impressive rather than because the reader needs it.
