# PRD Writing

## Overview

PRD writing creates a living alignment document for product, design, engineering, and review. It optimizes for useful decisions, executable scope, and testable acceptance.

## Use When

- The user asks for a PRD, feature spec, MVP scope, requirement review, user stories, or acceptance criteria.
- The team needs shared understanding of problem, users, scope, decisions, risks, and validation.
- The output will guide implementation or product review.

Do not use for public articles, technical references, tutorials, or general prose cleanup.

## Core Questions

Answer these before drafting:

- What problem is being solved?
- Who has the problem, in what scenario, and how often?
- What is in scope, out of scope, and deferred?
- What decisions have been made, and what remains open?
- How will the team know the requirement is satisfied?

## Output Pattern

For a PRD or review package, include:

- Background and problem statement.
- Goals and non-goals.
- Users, scenarios, and jobs to be done.
- Requirements grouped by priority.
- Scope boundaries and tradeoffs.
- Acceptance criteria that can be checked.
- Risks, dependencies, and open questions.
- Review notes for decisions still needed.

## Quality Bar

Priority order:

```text
用 > 真 > 清晰 > 完整 > 美 > 像
```

Check before finalizing:

- The problem is not replaced by a feature list.
- Requirements are decision-ready and implementation-ready.
- Acceptance criteria are observable or testable.
- Uncertainty is exposed instead of hidden.
- The document contains just enough context for collaboration.

## Hard Failures

- Expanding scope to look complete.
- Writing vision without acceptance criteria.
- Treating assumptions as confirmed requirements.
- Omitting non-goals, risks, or open questions.
